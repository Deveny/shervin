STATION S14

As human beings we are beyond the control of technology. We gain more and more confidence in the virtual world, even more so than in our fellow man. Technology takes over. Presently, we even transfer the visible errors in the online systems to the reality we live in. This is the result of the blurring scene between the physical, real world and the virtual world. By combining the physical and the digital, we can create new ideas to get a broader view of the world.
Technology provides a more individual world where people don’t have to leave their houses anymore. There is less and less talk of a physical community, everything is done digitally or virtually. That’s why this station tries to turn the digital features into a reality whereby the virtual world is taken out of this private sphere and can be viewed more like something shared.
The function of the station is to make people aware of what is happening around them in the world. What can we see as correct and what is actually incorrect? Is there just one truth or are we unknowing and are there different truths? We all try to work together for a better society, a better co-operation and a more accurate truth. This is only possible when we search for different ideas from different perspectives. We should try to be as open as possible to other ways of thinking and also see alternative approaches. Therefore, this station is open to opinions and information from different angles. This ensures that it can be viewed as a station for free expression. In every community, there is a station. This guarantees that there are people from different groups that have the ability to share their information with the other. They insert new information into the system, but they can also respond to other peoples information. All the information is stored in an online archive/ database where nothing can be lost. In this way, there is always an input and an output of the passer-by, and a certain democracy is present.

BAUHAUS: LABYRINTH STAGE

When you are standing outside of the Labyrinth you will have a very static feeling, but when you go inside of it you feel the dynamic forms, the moving projections, the overwhelming experiences. You expect something totally different standing on the outside. We want people to have the feeling that they are walking into a new world, into a new scene, a collage, where they will have the feeling that they are part of a very important scene.
We worked with specific scenographies. While walking through it, people can create their own performance. We employed ropes, sounds, different music and artists. At the outside we projected some little details from the inside to make people standing outside curious. At every entrance you have the feeling that it will be endless, because mirrors are placed on the inside walls, also at the crosspoints we will work with a foil to evoke a sense of confusion within the participant. This all will happen live.
The stage will be at the same level as the place where the visitors are standing. It will not be easy to find, which is also part of the game. People have to go inside the Labyrinth and there they will be led to the core, the actual stage, while they pass through different rooms where they have to do various exercises.
There will be not just be one artist playing. In the Labyrinth you will find different rooms with various sorts of experimental music. Some of them will be live, others will be on screens and others will be accessible through headphones.

ENSEMBLE: 'an architecture of the inbetween'

This research has evolved into a very personal reflection. I have tried to translate my state of mind and personal experiences into different spaces. I chose to design different spaces that were embraced together into one whole. A whole of different cultures, cultures from the East and the West, cultures that enrich each other, confirm each other, embed each other and form a unity.
A reality is exposed from a subjective perspective, a very pure form of expression. This honesty returns in the materiality of the architectural models. The transparency in the work has multiple meanings. First and foremost it shows the hybrid of both cultures and reflects the complete sincerity through its transparency and open character. It gives the viewer the freedom to project himself into the work, because there are no actual materials present. The materiality has been ‘taken away’ so that each person can add his / her own identity. The aspect of censorship also plays an important role. The transparent objects censor themselves, but exhibit their surroundings by leaving the entire layering open and exposed. Everything is transparent, so there are no secrets. The work does not get an imposed environment. Just like an inbetween person who is lost and adapts to his / her environment everywhere, the work will also adapt to the environment and it will be completed by the landscape that surrounds the work.
It developed into a narrative process. By combining various references from both cultures, the ‘inbetween’ arose. A melange of different architectural styles, a transculture, a hybrid of two civilizations. An amalgamation of two distant worlds. Both worlds engaged in a dialogue. A quest of mixed feelings, space where discussion is possible; where is my real identity now? I did not get one answer, but multiple answers. A “transcultural” person does not necessarily have to choose one side, we are here for a reason. We are here to connect, just as water is a connecting element in our world, we are there to connect people with each other.

REVERSE PERSPECTIVE

A work about confrontations and fears. Linking memories together through compositions. Everything revolves around design and aesthetics, intrinsic tranquility.
Objects are kept cool by means of foil. They stay close together. A coolness emanates towards the outside world, but a warmth remains within the object. Deepening in the togetherness of the materials. Finding a way to let nature flow within hand-kneaded objects.
The process of the material is being investigated. Is it breathing?
It involves uncertainty, fragility. Leaving traces, scars. Reading the past. The canvas becomes an echo of the objects in which the release is slow.
